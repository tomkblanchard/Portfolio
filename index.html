About Me

After working in mental health and disability services for over 8 years, I decided to make a career change. I wanted to get a tech related degree and use it to continue to support people with disabilities and mental health challenges. Now, after graduating from the University of Washington with a degree in Applied Computing, I am excited to contribute to the world of digital accessibility. My passion is developing websites and user experiences with accessibility in mind. I have the most experience with HTML, CSS and JavaScript, but I am also familiar with other languages including Java, Python and MySQL; and I have UX Design experience, too. I bring creativity to design challenges, and love to tackle complex problems and collaborate with others!

Some of my favorite things to do outside of work are cooking, long boarding, swimming, and training my new German Shepard, Kyra!
